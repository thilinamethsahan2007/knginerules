General Rules & Regulations

  • Eligibility: Open to all school students from Grade 9 to Grade 13.
  • Registration Deadline: All participants must register before 8th February 2026.
  • Registration Fee: FREE for all competitions.
  • Identification: Participants must carry their school ID card on the event day.
  • Punctuality: Late arrivals may be disqualified at the discretion of the organizing committee.
  • Code of Conduct: All participants must maintain respectful behavior. Any form of misconduct, harassment, or unsportsmanlike behavior will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Plagiarism Policy: Zero tolerance for copying, using others' work, or any form of cheating. Violators will be disqualified and may be barred from future events.
  • AI Tools: Use of AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, GitHub Copilot, etc.) is STRICTLY PROHIBITED in all competitions unless explicitly stated otherwise.
  • Decision Authority: The judges' decision is FINAL and BINDING. No appeals will be entertained.
  • Theme Compliance: All submissions must align with the Watch Dogs: Legion theme — featuring elements of hacking, resistance, surveillance, cyberpunk aesthetics, or digital revolution.
  • Disqualification: Teams/individuals may be disqualified for: cheating, plagiarism, late submissions, violation of rules, misconduct, or failure to follow instructions.
  • Technical Issues: The organizing committee is not responsible for any technical issues on the participant's end.

Competition Details

🎨 DesignerX

Individual • Online Submission

"Design is the silent ambassador of your resistance. Create visuals that hack the system."

THEME: Join the Resistance — DedSec Recruitment

Design a recruitment poster for DedSec, the hacktivist group from Watch Dogs: Legion. Your design should inspire people to join the digital resistance against surveillance and oppression.

ELIGIBILITY

  • Open to individuals in grades 9-13.
  • This is an individual competition, not for teams.
  • No limitations on participants from a single school.
  • Only ONE submission accepted per contestant.

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Software: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Figma, Canva, or GIMP.
  • Dimensions: A3 size (297mm × 420mm) or 4:5 scale (1080 x 1350px).
  • You must regularly capture screenshots of your work in progress.
  • Templates and plagiarism are strictly prohibited — immediate disqualification.
  • Design must have consistent theme aligned with Watch Dogs: Legion aesthetics.

SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

  • Submit all project files (textures, images, overlays) as a single .zip file.
  • Final artwork exported as .png, .jpg, or .pdf (minimum 300 DPI).
  • Include a PDF with layer screenshots + at least 5 in-progress screenshots.
  • File Naming: DesignerX_SchoolName_YourName.zip
  • Submit via official KNGINE'26 registration form.
  • Submissions after deadline will NOT be accepted.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Criteria Weightage
Creativity & Originality 25%
Theme Alignment (Watch Dogs: Legion) 25%
Visual Appeal & Aesthetics 20%
Technical Execution 20%
Presentation & Documentation 10%

💻 Codecom

Individual • Online

"Every line of code is a weapon. Hack the challenge. Own the leaderboard."

ELIGIBILITY

  • Students from grade 9-13 can participate.
  • This competition is only for individuals, not teams.
  • Solutions must be original. Code plagiarism = disqualification.

COMPETITION FORMAT

  • Platform: Online coding platform (link shared after registration).
  • Duration: Set time limit to solve all problems (announced before event).
  • Languages: Python, C++, JavaScript, Java, or C only.
  • Problem Difficulty: Easy, Medium, and Hard questions.

RULES

  • Must be registered via Google Form to participate.
  • Each problem has test cases; score based on passed test cases.
  • Multiple submissions allowed — highest score counted.
  • Ranking by total score. Ties broken by submission time.
  • No penalty for wrong submissions.
  • AI tools, code generators, external help = STRICTLY PROHIBITED.
  • Standard language documentation is allowed.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Criteria Weightage
Correctness of Solution 40%
Code Efficiency & Optimization 30%
Time Taken to Solve 20%
Code Readability 10%

🌐 Web Development

Team (2-4 Members) • Online Submission

"Build the digital resistance. Your website is the battleground."

TASK: Develop a DedSec Operations Dashboard

Create a futuristic web interface for hacktivist operatives featuring cyberpunk aesthetics, mission briefings, operative profiles, surveillance feeds, or resistance updates.

ELIGIBILITY

  • Students from grade 9-13 can participate.
  • Team size: 2 to 4 members per team.
  • Projects must be original. Plagiarism = disqualification.

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Technologies: HTML, CSS, JavaScript. Frameworks (React, Vue, Bootstrap, Tailwind) allowed.
  • Use descriptive variable/function names. Include comments for readability.
  • Upload code to a public GitHub repository.
  • Commit changes regularly to track progress.
  • Backend not required but awards extra points.
  • Extra points for responsiveness and additional functionalities.

SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

  • Complete source code in a ZIP file.
  • Hosted link (GitHub Pages, Netlify, Vercel) — highly recommended.
  • README file with team details and setup instructions.
  • File Naming: WebDev_SchoolName_TeamName.zip
  • Only FIRST submitted version will be evaluated.
  • Submissions after deadline will NOT be accepted.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Criteria Weightage
Functionality 25%
Design & User Experience 25%
Theme Alignment & Creativity 20%
Code Architecture & Cleanliness 15%
Responsiveness 15%

🎮 Cyber Combat

Team (5 Players) • Online

"The battlefield is digital. The stakes are real. Show no mercy."

GAME: VALORANT

5v5 Tactical Shooter — Tournament bracket style competition.

TEAM ELIGIBILITY

  • Open to all school students.
  • Teams: 5 main players from the same school.
  • Optional: 1 substitute (not mandatory).
  • All team members must provide names and details during registration.
  • Team members cannot be changed on match day unless pre-approved.

REGISTRATION RULES

  • Players must use their own Valorant accounts. No account sharing.
  • Register with team name + full names + Riot IDs of all players.
  • Registration must be completed by deadline.
  • All registered players must be present on tournament day.

MATCH FORMAT

  • Game Mode: 5v5, Unrated.
  • Best of 1 or Best of 3 depending on stage.
  • Map Selection: Teams participate in map banning phase.
  • First team to win 13 rounds wins. Overtime if tied (12-12).

CODE OF CONDUCT

  • No toxicity, abusive language, or harassment tolerated.
  • Cheating/hacking of any kind = immediate disqualification.
  • Sportsmanship mandatory. Respect opponents and teammates.
  • Teams must arrive 15 minutes before scheduled match.
  • Failure to show within 10 minutes = match forfeited.

📅 TOURNAMENT SCHEDULE

Tournament bracket and match schedule will be shared after registration closes.

🧠 Intellect

Team (3 Members) • On-Site

"Knowledge is power. Intelligence is your greatest hack."

INTER-SCHOOL IT & TECH QUIZ COMPETITION

Test your knowledge in ICT, programming, cybersecurity, and the world of Watch Dogs: Legion!

ELIGIBILITY

  • Each school allowed to send ONE team.
  • Team size: Maximum 3 students per team.
  • All team members must be students at the participating school.
  • Registration: Register at your school (not online). Deadline: 6th Feb 2026.

COMPETITION ROUNDS

  • Round 1 (Written MCQ - Elimination): Multiple Choice + Short Answer. Top teams advance.
  • Round 2 (Rapid Fire): Quick questions with time limits.
  • Round 3 (Buzzer Round - Finals): Top teams compete in buzzer format.

TOPICS COVERED

  • A/L and O/L ICT syllabus
  • Programming concepts
  • Cybersecurity basics
  • Tech industry & current affairs
  • Famous tech personalities & companies
  • Watch Dogs: Legion themed questions

RULES

  • No cheating or consulting external resources.
  • Mobile phones, smartwatches, electronic devices = STRICTLY PROHIBITED.
  • Participants must remain silent when not answering.
  • Shouting answers out of turn = negative marking.
  • Each question has a time limit. Late answers not considered.

SCORING

Round Points
Round 1 (MCQ) +1 per correct (no negative)
Round 2 (Rapid Fire) +2 per correct
Round 3 (Buzzer) +5 correct / -2 wrong
