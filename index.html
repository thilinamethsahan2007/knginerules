About KNGINE'26

KNGINE'26 is the annual Inter-School ICT Day organized by the Richmond College IT Society (RITS). This year's event embraces the Watch Dogs: Legion theme — hacktivism, resistance, and digital revolution. Join us as we unleash the power of technology through thrilling competitions.

General Rules & Regulations

  • Eligibility: Open to all school students from Grade 9 to Grade 13.
  • Registration Deadline: All participants must register before 8th February 2026.
  • Registration Fee: FREE for all competitions.
  • Identification: Participants must carry their school ID card on the event day.
  • Punctuality: Late arrivals may be disqualified at the discretion of the organizing committee.
  • Code of Conduct: All participants must maintain respectful behavior. Any form of misconduct, harassment, or unsportsmanlike behavior will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Plagiarism Policy: Zero tolerance for copying, using others' work, or any form of cheating. Violators will be disqualified and may be barred from future events.
  • AI Tools: Use of AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, GitHub Copilot, etc.) is STRICTLY PROHIBITED in all competitions unless explicitly stated otherwise.
  • Decision Authority: The judges' decision is FINAL and BINDING. No appeals will be entertained.
  • Theme Compliance: All submissions must align with the Watch Dogs: Legion theme — featuring elements of hacking, resistance, surveillance, cyberpunk aesthetics, or digital revolution.
  • Disqualification: Teams/individuals may be disqualified for: cheating, plagiarism, late submissions, violation of rules, misconduct, or failure to follow instructions.
  • Technical Issues: The organizing committee is not responsible for any technical issues on the participant's end.

Competition Details

🎨 DesignerX

Individual • Online Submission

"Design is the silent ambassador of your resistance. Create visuals that hack the system."

THEME

Join the Resistance — DedSec Recruitment.

Design a recruitment poster for DedSec (from Watch Dogs: Legion) to inspire a digital resistance against surveillance.

PARTICIPATION DETAILS

  • Eligibility: Grades 9-13
  • Format: Individual only (no teams)
  • School Limit: No limit per school
  • Submission Limit: One submission per contestant

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Software: Photoshop, Illustrator, Figma, Canva, or GIMP
  • Dimensions: A3 (297mm × 420mm) or 4:5 (1080 × 1350px)
  • Integrity: Plagiarism and templates are strictly prohibited
  • Process: Regular work-in-progress screenshots required

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

  • Platform: Official KNGINE'26 registration form
  • Format: Single .zip file containing:
  • All project files (textures, images, overlays)
  • Final artwork (.png / .jpg / .pdf, minimum 300 DPI)
  • PDF with layers + at least 5 in-progress screenshots
  • File Naming: DesignerX_SchoolName_YourName.zip

JUDGING CRITERIA

Criteria Weightage
Creativity & Originality 25%
Theme Alignment (Watch Dogs: Legion) 25%
Visual Appeal & Aesthetics 20%
Technical Execution 20%
Presentation & Documentation 10%

🌐 Web Development

Team (2-4 Members) • Online Submission

"Build the digital resistance. Your website is the battleground."

TASK

Amal is a full-stack web developer tasked by his company to create a web-based platform to combat the growing wave of cybercrime. The system should allow users to report suspicious activities and incidents, enabling the organization to monitor, track, and respond to cyber threats efficiently. The challenge lies in designing a secure, reliable, and responsive solution that addresses the increasing sophistication of online criminal activity while providing a clear and intuitive interface for reporting and managing incidents.

PARTICIPATION DETAILS

  • Eligibility: Grades 9-13
  • Team Size: 2-4 members
  • Integrity: Plagiarism results in disqualification

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Core Tech: HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Frameworks: React, Vue, Bootstrap, Tailwind, nextjs
  • Coding Standards: Descriptive names and proper comments
  • Version Control: Public GitHub repository with regular commits
  • Bonus Points: Backend integration, responsiveness, extra features

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

  • Submit the github repository link.
  • README with team details & setup instructions
  • Hosted link (Vercel, Netlify, GitHub Pages recommended)
  • Any commit after the deadline will not be considered
  • Only the first submission will be evaluated. No late submissions.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Criteria Weightage
Functionality 25%
Design & User Experience 25%
Theme Alignment & Creativity 20%
Code Architecture & Cleanliness 15%
Responsiveness 15%

Database integration will boost marks

🎮 Cyber Combat

Team (5 Players) • Online

"The battlefield is digital. The stakes are real. Show no mercy."

GAME: VALORANT (5v5 Tactical Shooter)

Format: Tournament bracket style

TEAM & ELIGIBILITY

  • Eligibility: Open to all school students
  • Roster: 5 Main Players (same school), 1 Substitute (optional)
  • Requirements: Names, details, and Riot IDs for all players must be provided at registration
  • Match Day: No roster changes unless pre-approved

REGISTRATION & CONDUCT

  • Account Rules: Players must use their own Valorant accounts; account sharing is prohibited
  • Attendance: Teams must arrive 15 minutes before their scheduled match
  • Forfeit Policy: Failure to show within 10 minutes results in a forfeit
  • Behavior: Zero tolerance for toxicity or harassment
  • Anti-Cheat: Any cheating leads to immediate disqualification

MATCH FORMAT

  • Mode: 5v5, Unrated
  • Structure: Best of 1 (BO1) or Best of 3 (BO3) depending on stage
  • Victory Condition: First team to 13 rounds wins
  • Overtime: If score reaches 12-12, overtime rules apply
  • Map Selection: Determined via a Map Banning Phase between teams

TOURNAMENT SCHEDULE

Specific brackets and match timings will be distributed via official channels after registration closes.

🧠 Intellect

Team (3 Members) • On-Site

"Knowledge is power. Intelligence is your greatest hack."

INTER-SCHOOL IT & TECH QUIZ COMPETITION

Test your knowledge in ICT, programming, cybersecurity, and the world of Watch Dogs: Legion!

PARTICIPATION & REGISTRATION

  • Eligibility: Open to students of participating schools
  • School Limit: One team per school
  • Team Size: Maximum of 3 students per team
  • Registration: Must be done at your school (not online)
  • Deadline: 6th February 2026

COMPETITION ROUNDS

  • Round 1 (Written MCQ): Multiple Choice and Short Answer questions; top teams advance
  • Round 2 (Rapid Fire): Quick-fire questions with strict time limits
  • Round 3 (Buzzer Round): Finals; top teams compete in live buzzer format

TOPICS COVERED

  • A/L and O/L ICT syllabus
  • Programming concepts
  • Cybersecurity basics
  • Tech industry & current affairs
  • Famous tech personalities & companies
  • Watch Dogs: Legion themed questions

RULES & SCORING

  • Prohibited Items: Mobile phones, smartwatches, and all electronic devices
  • Conduct: Silence is mandatory; shouting out of turn results in negative marking
  • Timing: Answers submitted after the time limit will not be considered
Scoring Table
Round Points
Round 1 (MCQ) +1 per correct answer (No negative marks)
Round 2 (Rapid Fire) +2 per correct answer
Round 3 (Buzzer) +5 per correct / -2 per wrong answer
